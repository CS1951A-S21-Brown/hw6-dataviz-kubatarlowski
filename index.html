2. List 3 reasons why D3 was helpful and improved your visualization (6 points)

a. The use of callbacks within D3 functions makes it easy to clean data for the visualization, preventing the need for preprocessing and uploading different csv files for different visualizations.

b. D3 gives a lot of option to customize the chart, from color schemes to the size and placement of the text. This allows you to fit the graph to the data and no the other way around (for example using legends insetad of crowded axes)

c. Different D3 charts follow similar workflows, making switching between different charts fairly intuitive. Most charts will start by referencing svg, cleaning data, appending the data, and then appending relevant information.

3. List 3 reasons why D3 would not be the best tool for creating a visualization (6 points)

a. D3 requires you to customize every aspect of the chart, leading to single visualizations taking a long time to create. Many libraries will automatically place the x and y axes, the title, and label the ticks.

b. The customizability of D3 makes each chart largely unreusable as you have to select the specific values for each axis.

c. The amount of customizability also makes D3 difficult to use with a team, as every aspect can be manipulated. A large project with many D3 charts could easily become unmaintanable as people change components and cannot trace the root of the issue
